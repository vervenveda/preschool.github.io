Feelings Story

Sometimes feelings get big…

We can calm down.

Step 1 · Notice the big feeling
Big feelings live in small bodies.
Sometimes my feelings get very big inside my small body. My tummy wiggles, my face feels warm, or my hands make tight fists. Big feelings are not bad — they are just loud and need a little help.
Grown-up note: Invite your child to point to where they feel big feelings — tummy, chest, face, hands.

Little Acts of Friendship

A gentle story about saying hello, sharing, listening, and being there for the friends we love.

Friendship Story

What is a friend?

Page 1 of 7
Step 1 · Meet a friend
Friends are people we care about.
A friend is someone we like to be with. We feel safe, silly, or cozy near them. Friends can be kids, grown-ups, or even pets — anyone we care about and who cares about us.
Grown-up note: Ask your child, “Who feels like a friend to you?” and let them name people, pets, or even stuffed animals.
